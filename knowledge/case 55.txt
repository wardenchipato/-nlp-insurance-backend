Document 8: 2024 Festive Season Report (December 15-26, 2024)
More deaths, fewer accidents — a deadly paradox

December 2024 presented a disturbing paradox. The Zimbabwe Republic Police recorded 1,211 accidents during the festive period (December 15-26) — down from 1,454 in 2023. Fewer crashes should mean fewer deaths. It did not.

Fatalities rose from 393 to 401. Injuries stayed flat at 401. The data told a grim story: when accidents happened in 2024, they were more likely to kill.

The deadliest days were public holidays. December 22 saw 106 accidents and 7 deaths. December 25 (Christmas) recorded 104 accidents and 13 deaths. December 26 (Boxing Day) had 87 accidents and 11 deaths. Three days accounted for 31 deaths — nearly 8% of the entire holiday toll.

What made 2024 more lethal? Higher speeds. More head-on collisions. More pedestrians struck at night. The UN has identified all of these as preventable with political will and enforcement.

The cost of inaction is a family mourning on Christmas morning, a mother buried on Boxing Day, and a nation that has normalized 400 holiday deaths.

Until behaviour changes, the paradox will continue: fewer accidents, but more graves.
