Document 4: Easter 2021 Statistics & Harare-Mutare Truck Crash (April 2021)
Nine seniors, one burst tyre, no second chance

Easter 2021 was devastating. Twenty-six people died on Zimbabwe's roads — more than triple the eight killed during Easter 2020. Total accidents rose from 178 to 231, and injuries jumped from 22 to 149.

But one crash captured the nation's heartbreak. On April 6, 2021, along the Harare-Mutare highway, an Isuzu KB truck carrying nine senior citizens suffered a tyre burst. The driver lost control. The vehicle overturned. All nine passengers died.

Their names: Elliot Mupfudze, Taurai Manjengwa, Isabel Madhuro, Fortunate Chihota, Lucy Bwerinofa, Margret Chikukwa, Revai Chishiri, Nyengeterai Mhike, and Rudo Mutema. Grandparents. Great-aunts. Community elders. Gone because of a preventable mechanical failure.

Globally, tyre condition is one of the most overlooked vehicle safety features. The UN recommends regular checks, but on Zimbabwe's highways, enforcement is weak. The ZRP itself later warned motorists to check tyre conditions before long journeys — but for nine families, that warning came too late.

The cost of inaction is measured in missed birthdays, empty pews, and nine graves dug in one week.
