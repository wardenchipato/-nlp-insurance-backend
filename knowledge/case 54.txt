Document 7: Easter 2023 Statistical Report (April 2023)
Fewer deaths, but still too many

Easter 2023 brought cautious optimism. Road deaths fell sharply — from 72 killed in Easter 2022 to just 30 in Easter 2023. This 58% drop occurred even as total accidents rose slightly from 264 to 288.

What changed? Police Commissioner-General Godwin Matanga announced enhanced deployments and a crackdown on illegal pirate taxis — "mushikashika" — ahead of the 2024 Easter period. Enforcement, it seemed, worked.

But 30 families still buried their dead. Pedestrians accounted for a significant portion — hit while walking on highways, crossing at non-intersections, or simply standing by the roadside.

Globally, pedestrians, cyclists, and motorcyclists account for more than 50% of road deaths. In Zimbabwe, that figure is likely higher, especially in marginalized communities where foot travel is the only option.

The UN says simple actions — slower speeds, seatbelts, avoiding phone use — can reduce fatalities. Easter 2023 proved that enforcement works. But until every driver slows down, 30 deaths will never be zero.

The cost of inaction is a highway stained with pedestrian blood.
