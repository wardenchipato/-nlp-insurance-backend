Document 9: 2025 Festive Season Report (December 15-26, 2025)
A catastrophic surge — the worst in years

The 2025 festive season was a catastrophe. The Zimbabwe Republic Police recorded 2,412 accidents between December 15 and 26 — double the 1,211 accidents from 2024. Fatal accidents rose from 65 to 87. Deaths climbed from 77 to 100. Injuries increased from 401 to 471.

Every metric worsened. Dramatically.

The worst single incident occurred on December 17, 2025, along the Harare-Nyamapanda Road at the 185km peg near Suswe. A Honda Fit carrying ten occupants encroached into oncoming traffic and collided head-on with a haulage truck. All ten people in the Honda Fit died.

Police Commissioner Paul Nyathi identified the causes: head-on collisions from encroaching into oncoming lanes, dangerous overtaking, excessive speeding, tyre bursts (especially on Toyota Hiace kombis), overloading, and pedestrians distracted by earphones or mobile phones.

Globally, speed is a factor in one-third of all fatal crashes. In Zimbabwe, it is the leading cause. The UN's goal of cutting road deaths by 50% by 2030 seems distant when a single festive season claims 100 lives.

The cost of inaction is ten bodies pulled from a Honda Fit, a kombi driver who never saw the truck, and a nation that has accepted 2,400 accidents as normal.
