Document 5: ZETDC Truck Crash (April 18, 2022)
Independence Day tragedy

April 18, 2022 was supposed to be a celebration of Zimbabwe's independence. Instead, at 9:45 PM on the Makuti-Kariba Road, a Nissan UD70 truck belonging to ZETDC — the national power utility — crashed. Four people died. Thirty-two were injured.

The crash was part of a catastrophic Easter weekend that claimed 48 lives across four major accidents. A Chimanimani bus crash killed 38. A Nyanga-Rwenya kombi crash killed four. A Stallion Cruise bus on the Bulawayo-Victoria Falls highway killed two. And the ZETDC truck killed four more.

Police Commissioner Paul Nyathi called the weekend "a bad one" for road deaths, expressing deep concern that "we are seeing people dying every day on our roads."

Night-time driving, driver fatigue, and poor visibility were all factors. The ZETDC crash occurred in darkness, on a rural highway, involving a utility vehicle whose driver had been on the road for hours.

The cost of inaction is written on Independence Day itself — a day of national pride forever stained by four unnecessary deaths.
