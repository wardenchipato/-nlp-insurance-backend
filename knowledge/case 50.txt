Document 3: 2020-2021 Festive Season Report (Dec 15, 2020 – Jan 2, 2021)
A season of sorrow

The festive season is supposed to be a time of joy. For 102 Zimbabwean families, the 2020-2021 holidays ended in grief.

From December 15 to January 2, the Zimbabwe Republic Police recorded 1,546 road accidents nationwide — up from 1,406 the previous year. While fatal accidents dropped from 74 to 67, the human toll remained brutal: 102 killed, 398 injured.

Two crashes defined this period. On Christmas Day 2020, a commuter omnibus collided head-on with a Toyota Hilux on the Mvuma-Gweru Road, killing six. Two days later, on December 27, an Isuzu KB and a BMW crashed head-on at the 130km peg in Mutoko, killing 17 people.

What caused this carnage? Speeding. Drunk driving. Following too close. Reckless overtaking. Wet roads from summer rains. The UN has identified all of these as preventable. Zimbabwe's roads, however, tell a different story.

The cost of inaction is 102 empty chairs at 2021's Christmas dinner. Until driver behaviour changes, the festive season will remain a season of sorrow.
