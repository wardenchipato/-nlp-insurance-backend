Document 2: Christmas Day 2019 Statistical Report (December 25, 2019)
A quiet Christmas, but not quiet enough

Christmas Day 2019 offered a rare glimmer of hope on Zimbabwe's deadly roads. The Zimbabwe Republic Police recorded 94 accidents nationwide — a 28% drop from the previous year's 131. Only three people died.

Globally, road traffic injuries are the leading cause of death for people aged 5 to 29. But on this single day, Zimbabwe bucked the trend. Police attributed the improvement to reduced speeding, fewer drunk drivers, and the absence of major bus crashes. Enforcement was visible: 138 touts arrested, 165 vehicles impounded, and 797 tickets issued.

Yet three families still buried their dead on Boxing Day morning. The causes were familiar — reversing errors, inattention, and a moment of distraction that turned fatal. The UN says simple actions — seatbelts, helmets, avoiding phone use — can save lives. On Christmas 2019, those actions nearly worked.

The cost of inaction is measured in what could have been. A zero-fatality Christmas is possible. Until then, three deaths is progress — but not victory.
