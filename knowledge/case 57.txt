Document 10: Easter 2026 Report & Masvingo Family Tragedy (April 2026)
Six family members, one head-on collision, zero survivors

Easter 2026 delivered a slight statistical improvement. Total accidents fell from 384 in 2025 to 337. Injuries dropped from 178 to 104. But deaths rose from 24 to 30 — and 63% of those killed were pedestrians.

One crash, however, transcended statistics.

On April 2, 2026, at approximately 9:50 AM on the Harare-Masvingo Road near Masvingo (246km peg), a Toyota Corolla and a Mercedes-Benz collided head-on. All six occupants of the Toyota Corolla died. They were all from the same family — residents of Tynwald North in Harare.

The dead: Lillian Mandla Mujuru (40), Nokutenda Mujuru (15), Makanaka Mujuru (13), Ronald Junior Mujuru (11), Rufaro Shalom Mujuru (7), and Kayden Mujuru (3).

A mother. A teenager. A 13-year-old. An 11-year-old. A 7-year-old. A toddler. Erased in a moment.

Two days later, on April 4, 2026, three fatal hit-and-run incidents occurred in Masvingo Province — all involving pedestrians. Police urged motorists to stop and render first aid, use warning triangles, and reduce speed when approaching vehicles with hazard lights. But for the Mujuru family, advice is meaningless.

The cost of inaction is six coffins, a motherless 3-year-old who died alongside his mother, and a road that has become a massacre site. Until Zimbabwe enforces speed limits and stops head-on collisions, the Mujuru family will not be the last.
