Tragic Toll: 15 Lives Lost In Heroes And Defense Holiday Accidents

In a somber report, the Zimbabwe Republic Police (ZRP) has revealed that a total of 15 individuals lost their lives, while 85 others sustained injuries in road traffic accidents during the recently concluded Heroes and Defence Forces holidays.

According to statistics released by ZRP national spokesperson Assistant Commissioner Paul Nyathi indicate that the number of fatal road and traffic accidents recorded during the August holidays in 2023 decreased to 132, compared to 163 incidents reported during the same period in 2022.

However, the number of fatalities during the 2023 Heroes and Defence Forces holidays tripled in comparison to the previous year, rising from 4 to 12.

Despite this increase, the overall number of fatalities during this year’s holidays was lower than the corresponding period in 2022, which recorded 15 deaths.

Assistant Commissioner Nyathi emphasized the need for caution, stating,

“The recorded statistics show a decrease in the number of accidents recorded from 163 to 132 and an increase in the number of people killed from 5 to 15 and the number of people injured from 29 to 85.”

The ZRP issued a stern reminder to motorists, urging them to exercise patience on the roads and avoid unsafe overtaking. They emphasized the importance of regular vehicle maintenance to ensure roadworthiness and the safety of passengers and goods.

Drivers were also cautioned against speeding in hazardous conditions and reminded to adhere to all road rules and regulations.

Motorists should also prioritise the servicing of vehicles to ensure they are fit and secure to carry passengers or goods on the roads.

Drivers should not speed in situations that are not safe to do so.

Above all, drivers should adhere to all road rules and regulations in order to curb the continuous loss of lives through road traffic accidents.

The continuous loss of lives through road traffic accidents remains a pressing concern, prompting authorities to call for heightened vigilance and responsible driving practices.
