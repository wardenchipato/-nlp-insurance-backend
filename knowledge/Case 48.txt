Mudzi Village Christmas Crash – 12 Killed (December 2019)
One village, twelve graves

Mudzi village in Mashonaland East is still counting its dead. On December 20, 2019, a single crash along the Harare-Nyamapanda Road took twelve lives — ten of them from one community.

Globally, 1.19 million people die annually on the world's roads, but in rural Zimbabwe, one accident can wipe out an entire extended family. The Mudzi victims included a 1-year-old infant, a 5-year-old child, and eight adults whose names now fill half the village cemetery.

The crash occurred at night — a darkness-related accident, with poor visibility cited by police. Reckless driving and dangerous overtaking were the suspected causes. Among the dead: William and Erica Chikowe, their children Mandi and Francis, plus relatives Erica Chari, Evelyn Kambizi, Christine Dawaka, infant Leeroy Kanetsa, Lenny Tsuro, Douglas Tsuro, plus Aaron Mutepfa (20) and Tashia Chapinduka (5).

On rural highways, where there are no streetlights and no barriers, night-time speeding is a death sentence. The UN says simple actions — slower speeds and avoiding dangerous overtaking — can cut fatalities by half by 2030. But for Mudzi village, that target comes twelve funerals too late.

The cost of inaction is written on every grave marker. Until driver behaviour changes on Zimbabwe's rural roads, villages like Mudzi will keep burying their children.
