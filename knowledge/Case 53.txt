Document 6: Heroes & Defence Forces Holidays 2022 + Harare-Masvingo Crash (August 2022)
Five dead, one infant survivor — but no one lived

There was some good news in August 2022. Road deaths during the Heroes and Defence Forces holidays dropped dramatically — from 20 killed in 2021 to just 3 in 2022. Improved roads and police awareness campaigns were credited.

But three days before the holiday period officially began, tragedy struck.

On August 6, 2022, at approximately 3:20 PM on the Harare-Masvingo Road (33km peg), a Nissan Note carrying a ZRP officer's family collided head-on with a Scania haulage truck. Five people died on the spot. One was seriously injured.

The victims: Nyaradzo Mugadza (49, driver, ZRP Chikurubi Camp), Simbarashe Murapa (44, Kuwadzana Extension), Gwenhere Tsitsi (47, Chikurubi Camp), Denis Mapikure (40, Hatfield), and Ethan David Chiyangwa — just 5 years old.

A 5-year-old boy, killed in a head-on collision caused by reckless driving or inattention. Police did not specify which. The distinction matters little to the families.

Globally, children aged 5 to 29 are the most at-risk group for road traffic injuries. Ethan David Chiyangwa is now a statistic. He should have been a student.

The cost of inaction is a child's future, erased in a moment of driver error.
